Current Project

I am currently conducting an undergraduate research project to study galaxy rotation curves using the IllustrisTNG cosmological simulation.

The main goal of this project is to test the large-scale behavior of galaxy rotation curves and the mass distribution of dark matter halos by directly analyzing simulated particle data.

In particular, this work is motivated by recent weak-lensing measurements reported in:

Mistele et al. (2024), "Indefinitely Flat Circular Velocities and the Baryonic Tully–Fisher Relation from Weak Lensing".

That study finds that galaxy circular velocities remain approximately constant out to radii of order ~1 Mpc. My project aims to verify this result using simulated galaxies in the IllustrisTNG dataset by:

  • Selecting isolated central galaxies (parent halos)
  • Downloading particle data within a radius of ~1 Mpc
  • Computing enclosed mass profiles M(<r)
  • Deriving circular velocity curves
  • Comparing simulation results with observational constraints
